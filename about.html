Our Story

About Squnga’esihle Trading

Background

Squnga’esihle Trading (Pty) Ltd was founded with a singular and purposeful mandate — to accelerate the capacity and market entry of emerging Implementing Agent companies in South Africa’s critical service delivery ecosystem. We are purpose-built for impact: equipped to deliver end-to-end solutions across the urban and rural housing sector while simultaneously extending our footprint into strategic engineering infrastructure projects.

Our engineering portfolio encompasses internal engineering services, local road access infrastructure, sports and recreation facilities, early childhood development centres (ECD/Crèche), and community hall developments — each project undertaken with the same rigour and commitment to excellence that defines everything we do.

At Squnga’esihle, we understand that true development goes beyond bricks and mortar. It is about people, communities, and the futures we collectively build. Every project we touch represents a community served, a family empowered, and a municipality transformed.

Track Record

Experience & Expertise

The leadership team brings over five years of hands-on, multi-disciplinary experience in the planning, management, and delivery of low-cost income subsidy housing projects across both urban and rural KwaZulu-Natal — a portfolio spanning thousands of units and a consistent pattern of delivery excellence across all project phases.

Project Management

Strategic project planning and scheduling, appointment and follow-up of professionals, packaging and submission of PLS applications to the Department of Human Settlements, tender briefing attendance and proposal compilation.

Social Facilitation

Stakeholder introduction, pre-planning of settlement layouts, CBP formation, constitution assistance, community and traditional authority workshopping on housing policies, Social Compact development, and Implementing Agent agreement management.

Beneficiary Administration

End-to-end completion and submission of subsidy application forms, active expediting of approvals with the Department of Human Settlements (DoHS), and meticulous record management throughout.
